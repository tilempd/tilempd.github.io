About

Obtained a Bachelor of Science degree in Physics at the University of Athens, advised by Professor Sarantis Sofianos. Subsequently, pursued a Master of Science in Physical Oceanography within the Ocean Physics and Modelling Group at the University of Athens and worked under the joint supervision of Professors Sarantis Sofianos and Peter Diamessis. Currently undertaking a Ph.D. in Environmental Engineering at Cornell University as a member of the Environmental C.F.D. Group, advised by Professor Diamessis.

Research focuses on the dynamics and transport induced by unstable internal solitary waves of depression shoaling over gentle slopes. Through the use of high-accuracy, high-resolution numerical simulations built with advanced spectral methods and complemented by Lagrangian diagnostics, this work aims to bridge the gap between large-scale flow dynamics and small-scale processes, linking detailed numerical modeling with observational studies and reproducible state-of-the-art simulation frameworks.
